Phase 3 Cardiology Rotation • Estimated time: 12–18 minutes

Introduction & Learning Objectives

By the end of this case, you will be able to:

  • Recognize the clinical presentation and diagnosis of AF.
  • Apply the 4‑stage AF classification system.
  • Assess stroke risk (CHA₂DS₂‑VASc) and choose anticoagulation.
  • Differentiate rate vs. rhythm control and identify candidates for early rhythm control.
  • Implement risk‑factor modification, focusing on obesity and sleep apnea.

Module Conclusion & Takeaways

  • Assess stroke risk: CHA₂DS₂‑VASc; DOACs are preferred (except mechanical valves or moderate–severe MS).
  • Early rhythm control: For new AF, early strategy can improve hard outcomes.
  • Treat the substrate: ≥10% weight loss and diagnose/treat OSA (e.g., CPAP) to reduce recurrence and improve ablation success.
